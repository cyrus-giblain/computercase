How common is visual hacking in public spaces?
A global study by 3M showed that nearly 90% of attempts to steal sensitive information through visual hacking were successful, demonstrating the ease with which information can be visually compromised.
How many workers are concerned about someone looking over their shoulder while they work in a public place?
A survey by the Ponemon Institute indicates that about 58% of mobile workers are concerned about onlookers viewing sensitive information on their screen while working in public.
What percentage of professionals work while traveling or in public spaces, thus exposing their screens to potential visual hacking?
A survey by Kensington found that approximately 70% of employed adults work from mobile devices in public spaces, such as airports, cafes, and co-working spaces, at least once per week, making them vulnerable to visual hacking.
